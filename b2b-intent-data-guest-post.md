## Understanding B2B intent data and how it works

B2B intent data comprises behavioral signals that reveal when prospects are actively researching solutions like yours. These digital breadcrumbs—website visits, content downloads, keyword searches, social media interactions—create a comprehensive picture of buying interest and readiness.

Modern intent data operates through three primary types:

- **First-party intent data** captures behavior on your owned properties: pricing page visits, whitepaper downloads, demo requests, and email engagement. This provides the most accurate signals but limited to prospects already aware of your brand.
- **Third-party intent data** aggregates behavioral signals across publisher networks, review sites, and online properties, identifying companies researching your solution category before they visit your website.
- **Second-party intent data** from platforms like G2 and TrustRadius reveals prospects actively comparing vendors—high-value signals indicating imminent purchase decisions.

The most sophisticated [intent data platforms](https://volarym.com) now use AI and machine learning to process billions of signals in real-time, moving beyond simple keyword matching to understand context, topic relevance, and buying stage. These systems assign intent scores based on:

1. Recency of behavior
2. Frequency of engagement
3. Content type consumed
4. Engagement depth

This enables precise prioritization of accounts most likely to convert.
